## Currency-rate par-junk fix (UNCOMMITTED — fixes user-reported "Divine price is off")
User screenshot (deployed VM) showed **Divine = 1.00 ex** (should be ~150) + a cascade: the hero "1.00 ex PER DIVINE", and Chaos/Annul/Chance/Fracturing rendered "in div" with the ex number unchanged because `home.js` divides by a `divineEx` of 1. Vaal & Gemcutter's Prism also read exactly 1.00 ex.
- **Root cause** (proven by a live probe of the exchange offers): the bulk Currency Exchange is littered with spam **"par" listings** that swap the base unit one-for-one — `1 exalted : 1 divine` (giving away ~165 ex), often with real stock (Divine's had stock 67). `bestExchangeOffer` sorted offers by price ascending and took the cheapest above the stock floor, so the par junk (ratio 1) beat the real floor (166-185 ex) for every above-1ex currency → rate = 1.
- **Fix** (`server.js`, `bestExchangeOffer` + new `robustCheapestOffer`): drop offers where `payAmount === receiveAmount` (the par signature) and take the cheapest *real* one; fall back to par only if that's all there is. No legitimate seller offers a par swap vs exalted. **Crucially NOT a median/outlier filter** — that was tried first and WRONGLY repriced sub-1ex high-liquidity currencies (Transmute 0.33→0.5, Aug 0.14→**4.95**) because the numerous par-junk offers drag the median up to 1; sub-1ex currencies already pick their true floor (below the par junk) so par-drop leaves them untouched.
- Benefits all three `bestExchangeOffer` callers (currency strip rate, arbitrage buy-leg — the par junk would otherwise fabricate a ~149ex fake Divine arbitrage, rune book).
- **Live-verified** via probe through the real `fetchExchangeChunked`/`bestExchangeOffer`: Divine 1→**166**, Vaal 1→**4**, GCP 1→**2**, Chaos **13** unchanged, Transmute **0.333** preserved. Regression test in `backfill-test.js` (+3 asserts: drops par for divine, keeps sub-1ex transmute floor, par-only fallback). smoke 85/85.
- **Note:** the local `.currency-rates.json` cache (and the VM's) will only show the corrected numbers after the next *successful* refresh — at fix time the shared Trade2 IP was rate-limited (from the diagnostic probes), so a forced refresh returned the stale cache. No code issue; it self-corrects once the limit window clears (~10 min) and the home ↻ / warmer repopulates. Deploy with `--build`.
